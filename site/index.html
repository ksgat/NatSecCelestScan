Kestrel

Kestrel is a compact visual-inertial localization unit designed to output GPS-style telemetry from cameras, IMU data, and ground-to-map matching when conventional positioning is unavailable, degraded, or intentionally omitted from the demo path.

What It Is

A fieldable localization prototype

Rig

The physical unit combines a downward-facing camera path, an IMU, stereo-derived scale, and a local compute node that emits navigation-style telemetry to a laptop dashboard.

Positioning Approach

Kestrel uses visual odometry for short-horizon motion, stereo for altitude and scale hints, and ground-to-map matching for absolute correction against pre-cached orthophoto imagery.

Demo Shape

The demo centers on a live map view, diagnostics, camera feeds, and NMEA output rather than claims of production-grade autonomy. The emphasis is on a coherent, inspectable technical stack.
